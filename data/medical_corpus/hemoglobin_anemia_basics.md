# Hemoglobin and Anemia – Educational Overview

## What is Hemoglobin?
Hemoglobin is a protein present in red blood cells. Its primary function is to transport oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body.

## Typical Reference Ranges (Adults)
- Female: approximately 12.0–15.5 g/dL
- Male: approximately 13.0–17.0 g/dL

Reference ranges may vary slightly between laboratories.

## What Does Low Hemoglobin Mean?
A hemoglobin value below the reference range is commonly referred to as anemia. Anemia indicates reduced oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood.

## Common Causes of Low Hemoglobin
- Iron deficiency
- Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
- Chronic blood loss
- Chronic kidney disease
- Bone marrow disorders

## Common Symptoms
- Fatigue
- Weakness
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Pale skin

## Typical Specialist Involved
- General Physician
- Hematologist (for persistent or severe anemia)
